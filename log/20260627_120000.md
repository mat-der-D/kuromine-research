### A. Lemma A numerical test (Wolfram)

Recall Lemma A (Session 20, UNPROVEN, possibly false): if the moving 3-adic depth
$\delta_x(a) = v_3(2^x + 5 - a^3) \ge j+1$, then $|\sigma_x(a) - 1| \ge c_0(j+1)/x$,
where $\sigma_x(a) = 3\log_2 a / x$. If true, this is incompatible with the genuine
size band ($|\sigma_x(A)-1| = O(1/x)$) for $k_y \ge 1$, giving a contradiction.

"$3^{d-2}$ law" of Test 1. So a target *designed* to have a small deep cube-root **does**
have one.

**Test 3 — real-regime check ($k_x = 1, k_y = 1$).** For the first surviving Theorem-5

**smaller** than the fixed-target law allows — exactly the contradiction Lemma A wants,
**but** Test 2 shows the law is a property of the *fixed* target, not the moving target
$N(x) = 2^x + 5$.

## Results

1. **Lemma A is REFUTED as a universal "depth $\Rightarrow$ size" transfer principle.**
   The empirical "smallest depth-$d$ integer $\approx 3^{d-2}$" law that *would* prove
   Lemma A is a property of the **fixed** target $N = 37$ only. Test 2 exhibits an explicit
   moving-style target $N = 22^3 + 3^{10}\cdot7$ whose smallest depth-10 integer is the
   small number 22. For the **actual** moving target $N(x) = 2^x + 5$, the existence of a
   small deep cube-root ($A$, size $2^{x/3}$, depth $j+1$) is *exactly the Kuromine
   question itself*. Lemma A is therefore **circular**: it can only be established by
   knowing whether $2^x+5$ has a small deep cube-root, which is what we are trying to
   decide. No place-coupling inequality bridges this without re-importing an effective
   irrationality measure (the very tool that is unavailable). **The crux lemma of the
   Coupling-Height framework cannot be proved; the framework collapses to the same
   two-comparable-S-units barrier.**

2. **Lemma B′ is REFUTED.** Since $\alpha = P/Q = 3\log2/\log3$ is irrational, Weyl's
   theorem makes $\{k_x\alpha + \beta\}$ equidistributed in $[0,1)$, so
   $\inf_{k_x\ge1} D_{\ge0}(k_x) = 0$ (not attained). The numerics confirm: the minimal
   nonnegative $D$ drops from 98.5 to **0.537 at $k_x = 84180$** and continues toward 0.
   Session 15's "$D \approx 55$ floor" was a finite-range artifact ($k_x \le 5000$). The
   $D$-lattice **does** approach the boundary $D = 0$ arbitrarily closely. Lattice
   Rigidity in the relativized form B′ is **false**.

   *Caveat (honest):* This does **not** revive bounded-$A$ elimination (Session 15
   dead-end). $D$ small means $A \approx 3e^{D}$ small *for that specific $(k_x,k_y)$*,
   but those pairs are sparse and at enormous $k_x$; and the bounded-$A$ contradiction
   was already closed because $A$ must equal a *small fixed* integer for the valuation
   argument, whereas even $D = 0.5$ gives $A \approx 3e^{0.5} \approx 5$ — but Theorem M
   forbids $D < 0$ and Theorem N forbids small fixed $A$ at depth $j+1$ unless $j$ is
   tiny. The near-boundary pairs are exactly the residual hard core; making $D$ small does
   not by itself eliminate them (the genuine $A$ at $D \approx 0.5$ would be $O(1)$ —
   but then $v_3(2^x+5-A^3)$ for that fixed small $A$ is a bounded constant, contradicting
   $j+1$; this is precisely the Theorem-N corollary, already known). So B′'s falsity
   neither helps nor hurts: it removes the only remaining structural prop under the
   Coupling-Height framework.
