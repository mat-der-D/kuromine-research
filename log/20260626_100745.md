### C. Structural placement of Kuromine relative to the decidability frontier (Wolfram)

A linear-exponential equation allows free variables in exponents of **fixed** bases and at
most **linearly** (degree 1) as polynomial terms. Kuromine $2^x+3^y+5=z^3$:

1. **Exponential skeleton over exactly two primes.** $2^x,3^y,5$ are linear-exponential over
   $\{2,3\}$ ($T=6$, two prime divisors) — inside the proven decidable prime-count.
2. **The cube $z^3$ has $z$ free at degree 3.** $z$ ranges over all integers and is not a
   power of a fixed base; the two known $z=2,4$ are powers of 2 by coincidence, but generic
   family $z\approx2^{x/3}$ is not $\{2,3\}$-smooth (Wolfram: smoothness check on $\{2,4\}$
   incidental), so $z$ cannot be re-encoded as a monomial on $\{2,3\}$.
3. **The cube persists after Theorem-5 factoring.** With $z=3^j+A$ (Wolfram):
   $$z^3 = 3^{3j} + 3^{1+2j}A + 3^{1+j}A^2 + A^3,$$
   so the free integer $A\sim2^{x/3}$ enters at **degree 3** ($A^3$) and degree 2 ($A^2$).

Summary (Wolfram):

| quantity | value |
|---|---|
| distinct primes in exponential part ($T$) | 2 ($T=6$) |
| max polynomial degree in a free variable | 3 (the cube) |
| max degree allowed in the decidable class | 1 (linear-exponential) |
| inside prime-count frontier? | yes ($2 \le 2$) |
| inside degree frontier? | **no** ($3 > 1$) |

## Results

1. **Kuromine is located precisely relative to the decidability frontier: one prime within,
   two polynomial degrees above.** Its exponential skeleton sits exactly where KLNOW proves
   decidability (two primes), but the free cube $z^3$ — degree 3 in a free variable — is two
   degrees above the degree-1 linear-exponential class. This is a **degree obstruction**,
   structurally distinct from (and orthogonal to) the two-comparable-S-units metric barrier
   that blocked Baker.

2. **The degree obstruction collapses onto a known wall.** A free degree-3 term per fixed RHS
   is a Mordell/Thue cubic — exactly the already-retired per-fixed-variable elliptic-curve /
   Thue-Mahler dead end: finite and effective per fixed $x$ (or $y$), but **no uniformity**
   across the infinite Theorem-5 family. So the decidability angle offers nothing new beyond
   confirming the wall.

3. **Even the in-class engine is dead here.** KLNOW's decidability proof relies on Baker's
   theorem — already structurally inapplicable to Kuromine (Session 5: the relevant $p$-adic
   linear forms have $v_p(\Lambda_p)=0$, so no Baker upper bound is ever violated).

4. **arXiv watch otherwise negative** — eleventh independent confirmation of the
   two-comparable-S-units barrier. No 2026 result placing a single free quadratic/cubic term
   inside the linear-exponential decidable class; that would be the genuinely new development
   that could touch Kuromine.

## Conclusion

The 2025–2026 decidability results for linear-exponential Diophantine systems (KLNOW;
Dong–Shafrir) do **not** reach Kuromine. The reason is now pinned with precision: Kuromine
lives **one prime inside but two polynomial degrees above** the proven frontier, and the
degree-3 free cube is exactly the per-fixed-variable Thue/Mordell wall already on record
(no family uniformity). Two structurally distinct barriers are now cleanly identified for
Kuromine:

- **(i) metric/analytic:** the two-comparable-S-units barrier — need an unconditional effective
  $\kappa<1.107$ for $|z^3-3^y|$ (no such tool);
- **(ii) decidability/degree:** the free cube puts Kuromine above the linear-exponential
  decidable class.

Both rest, at their core, on Baker being inapplicable in this regime. The honest deliverable
is a sharper map of *why* the newest decidability frontier stops short of Kuromine — useful
because it identifies a concrete future trigger: an extension of the KLNOW/Dong–Shafrir
decidability class to a single free higher-degree term would be the development to watch for.

No new theorem is claimed; this is a literature-placement and structural-diagnosis session.
The open target is unchanged.
