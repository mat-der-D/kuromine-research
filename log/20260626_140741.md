### B. New structural sharpening of the open target

The open target is $|z^3 - 3^y| > C\,(z^3)^\kappa$ with $\kappa < 1.107$ in the regime
$3^y \approx z^2$. I examined *what kind* of theorem this is, with Wolfram support.

- **Key observation: $\kappa < 1.107 < 2$ is below the Dirichlet floor.** Every irrationality
  measure $\mu$ of an irrational $\xi$ satisfies $\mu \ge 2$ (Dirichlet: infinitely many
  $|\xi - p/q| < q^{-2}$). An irrationality measure is a statement *"good approximations
  (exponent $\ge 2$) are not too good."* The Kuromine target asks for a bound with effective
  exponent **below 2** — so it is **categorically not an irrationality-measure statement.**
  Consequently the entire CDT-school program (effective irrationality measures for $2^{1/3}$)
  — even if it eventually drives $\mu$ for $2^{1/3}$ down toward its floor — **cannot reach
  the target**, because the target lives below the floor where measures say nothing.

- **CDT explicit-bound check (Wolfram).** CDT (arXiv:2510.04156) Thm 2.1 bounds
  $\mu_{\mathrm{eff}}(\sqrt[3]{a/b}) \le (35.1/\eta)\max\{(\log 2n)/(\eta\log a),10\}^2$.
  The method is strong only when $a/b$ is *close to 1*; for $2^{1/3}$ ($a=2$, far from 1) the
  parameter $\eta$ is small and the bound is large (even the bracket floor 10 with generous
  $\eta=1$ gives $\ge 3510$). This is the quantitative reason $2^{1/3}$ has effective
  $\mu > 2$ and not a sharp value — orthogonal to the target anyway by the sub-Dirichlet
  point above.

- **Realized approximation exponent (Wolfram, 50-digit), reconfirming S11.** For $k_x=1$,
  the realized exponent $\kappa_{\text{real}}(k_y) = -\log|2^{2/3} - z/W|/\log W$ of $z/W$ to
  $2^{2/3}$ is **negative for all $k_y \ge 1$**: $-0.585, -1.377, -2.170$ for $k_y=1,2,3$
  (the $k_y=0$ floor is $\approx 0.62$ per S11, hit precision limit here). So $z/W$ does not
  merely fail to be a *good* approximation — for the bulk of the family it **diverges** from
  $2^{2/3}$ (it is an *anti*-approximation). There is literally no approximation for a measure
  to constrain.

**Net new diagnostic statement (kept):** the open target is a **sub-Dirichlet gap/separation
inequality** ($\kappa < 2$), a kind of statement that lies entirely outside the
irrationality-measure paradigm. This explains, more sharply than before, *why* improving the
$2^{1/3}$ measure is structurally the wrong tool — not just insufficient in constant, but the
wrong category of theorem.
