### 1.1 The four structural facts, and what each one kills

Write the equation as a relation among four "atoms": $u = 2^x$, $v = 3^y$, the constant

*fixed small bases*; the only "power" with a varying base is the cube $z^3$, whose exponent
$3$ is *fixed*. Consequences:
- No Frey curve / modular method (needs a varying prime exponent to carry a mod-$p$ Galois
  representation). **Kills modular.**
- The variables $u, v$ are *not algebraic coordinates*; the constraint "$u = 2^x$, $x \in
  \mathbb Z$" is not cut out by polynomial equations. **Kills Chabauty-Kim and all
  algebraic-geometry integral-point machinery** (categorical mismatch, Session 13).

**(P2) Two comparable dominant S-units (the metric heart).** In the *surviving* regime
$2^x \gtrsim 3^y$ with $3^y \approx z^2$, the two S-units $u = 2^x$ and $v = 3^y$ are of
*comparable logarithmic size* ($\log u / \log v \to 1/(2\log_3 2) \approx 1.057$, Wolfram).
Neither S-unit is a small perturbation of the other, and neither linear form
$\Lambda_2 = x\log 2 - \log(z^3 - 3^y - 5)$, $\Lambda_3 = y\log 3 - \log(z^3 - 2^x - 5)$ is
forced small in *any* metric:
- **Archimedean:** the realized approximation exponent of $z/2^a$ to $2^{2/3}$ is
  $\kappa_{\text{real}} \approx 0.62$ at the family floor and goes *negative* off it
  (Session 11) — far below any irrationality measure $\mu \ge 2$, which only constrains
  *good* approximations. **Kills irrationality measures / Thue / Badziahin.**
- **$p$-adic ($p = 2, 3$):** $v_2(\Lambda_2) = v_3(\Lambda_3) = 0$ because $5 \equiv 2 \bmod
  3$ and $v_2(3^y + 5) = 3 = v_2(z^3)$. Yu's $p$-adic Baker upper-bounds $v_p$; with actual

**(P3) The additive constant $5$ (the smoothness-breaker).** The "$+5$" prevents the RHS
of every casting from being a single S-unit:

- The phantom's reductions match every congruence on $A, B$ ($A \equiv 3$, $B \equiv 37/3

### 1.2 The exact nature of the wall (precise characterization)

> **Diagnosis.** The Kuromine equation occupies a *single point of mutual failure* of two
> orthogonal families of tools, and the phantom certifies that the failure is not an
> artifact of any one casting:
>
> **(A) The metric/analytic family** (Baker, irrationality measures, Thue, Bugeaud,
> holonomy bounds, ABC) all deliver bounds of the form *"a linear form / approximation /
> S-unit difference is large unless it is the unique small one."* They are nontrivial only
> in a regime where **one** distinguished quantity is anomalously small (one dominant
> S-unit, one good rational approximation, one near-extremal linear form). Kuromine sits in
> the **two-comparable-S-units regime**, where there is **no distinguished small
> quantity** — $u$ and $v$ are co-equal, every relevant linear form has $v_p = 0$ and
> $\kappa_{\text{real}} < 1$. The analytic tools have *nothing to act on*. The precise
> deficit is the **effective-exponent gap** $0.631 < \kappa < 1.107$: tools give
> $\kappa \to 3^-$; the problem lives at $\kappa \approx 0.631$ and the contradiction
> threshold is $\kappa < 1.107 = 3 - 3\log_3 2$.
>
> **(B) The algebraic/local family** (Chabauty-Kim, modular, congruence, Skolem,
> primitive divisors) all require *either* an algebraic curve (so the variable set is cut
> out by polynomials) *or* a local obstruction (a modulus with no solution) *or* a varying
> prime exponent. Kuromine has **none**: its variables are exponents of fixed bases (no
> algebraic curve, no varying exponent), and the **phantom** is a global rational point
> that destroys every local obstruction simultaneously.
>
> **The conjunction (P1)∧(P2)∧(P3)∧(P4) is the wall.** It is not a quantitative gap to be
> closed by sharper constants; it is a *structural degeneracy*: the equation is
> "everywhere locally a solution, nowhere metrically extremal, and not algebraic." Every
> existing theory needs at least one of {algebraicity, a local obstruction, a varying
> exponent, a distinguished small analytic quantity}. Kuromine provides none.

**The one asymmetry the wall does NOT erase.** There is exactly one place where the genuine

## §2. SUB-STEP 2 — Framework Blueprinting

**Question:** given the diagnosis, what *kind* of new machinery could bypass the wall? I
deliberately do not default to any existing theory.

### 2.1 What the framework must NOT be

- Not a sharper analytic bound — the regime has no distinguished small quantity (P2); any
  bound of "X is large unless X is the small one" type is vacuous.
- Not a new local/congruence condition — the phantom satisfies every one (P4).
- Not an algebraic curve / Galois construction — there is no curve and no varying exponent
  (P1).

### 2.2 The one feature any winning framework must exploit

By §1.2 the *only* property distinguishing genuine solutions from the everywhere-locally-
present phantom is the **3-adic valuation growth** $v_3(2^x + 5 - A^3) = j + 1 \to \infty$
(Theorem N). A genuine solution forces $A$ (a global integer, $v_3(A) = 0$) to lie 3-
adically *extremely close* to the cube root branch of $2^x + 5$, to depth $j + 1$, while the
phantom lies at depth-1 only ($v_3(A_{\text{ph}} - \text{branch}) = $ low). So:

> **Blueprinting principle.** The genuine-vs-phantom distinction is a statement about how
> a **global integer** ($A$, or $z$) can simultaneously satisfy:
>   (i) an **archimedean size law** $A \asymp 2^{x/3}$ (real, P2), and
>   (ii) a **3-adic depth law** $v_3(2^x+5-A^3) = j+1$ (Theorem N), with $j+1$ *coupled to
>        the very same $x$* through the Theorem-5 lattice ($x = 5 + 332640k_x$, $j = 55439 +
>        55440 k_y$).
> The phantom satisfies an archimedean law and a *shallow* 3-adic law but **decouples** the
> two scales (its $j = -1$). A genuine solution must *couple* a large real size to a large
> 3-adic depth, on a single global integer, along a rigid arithmetic progression.

This is neither purely archimedean (kills analytic tools) nor purely 3-adic (kills local
tools): it is a **coupling between the archimedean place and the place $3$, indexed by an
arithmetic progression.** No existing theory measures this coupling because each existing
theory works one place at a time, and the phantom is invisible to each place separately.

### 2.3 What *type* of object captures a cross-place coupling indexed by a progression?

The natural mathematical home for "a single global object whose archimedean size and
$p$-adic depth are simultaneously constrained" is an **adelic / Arakelov height** — but the
*standard* height (sum of local contributions $\sum_v \log^+|\cdot|_v$) is exactly what gives
quality $0.888 < 1$ (P3) and is therefore blind to the problem. The standard height treats
the places *additively and independently*; the wall is precisely that no single place
carries information. **We need a height that is sensitive to the correlation between places,
not their sum.**

Therefore the proposed framework is a **new non-additive arithmetic height** — call it a
*coupling height* — together with the **filtration** of integers by 3-adic depth-to-a-moving-
target, and a **custom metric** in which the phantom and genuine solutions are separated
*because* the metric weights the archimedean–3-adic correlation. Concretely the three new
pieces are:

1. A **moving 3-adic target filtration** $\mathcal F = \{F_n\}$: stratify $\mathbb Z$ by
   $v_3$ of the distance to the (k_x-dependent) cube-root branch of $2^x + 5$.
2. A **coupling height** $\widehat H$ that multiplies (not adds) an archimedean size term by
   a 3-adic depth term, so that genuine solutions are forced to *large* $\widehat H$ while
   the phantom has *bounded* $\widehat H$.
3. A **progression-rigidity functional** measuring the obstruction to the real size and
   3-adic depth being simultaneously realizable by an integer of the lattice
   $x = 5 + 332640 k_x$.

**Why this is the most promising countermeasure to the specific wall:** the wall is a
single-place degeneracy plus a phantom. A height that is *non-additive across places* is the
minimal new structure that can (a) see information invisible to each place separately —
defeating P2/P4 — while (b) not requiring algebraicity or a varying exponent — sidestepping
P1 — and (c) being immune to the additive-quality computation that makes ABC vacuous —
sidestepping P3. The phantom's defining weakness ($j = -1$, shallow 3-adic depth) is exactly
a *small value of the depth factor*, so a multiplicative coupling height assigns it a small
height and separates it from genuine solutions, which carry depth $j + 1 \to \infty$.

## §3. SUB-STEP 3 — Axiomatic Definition

We define a new framework, the **Coupling-Height / Moving-Filtration framework** for the
Theorem-5 family. All objects are defined on the Theorem-5 lattice
$$\mathcal L = \{(x,y) : x = 5 + 332640\,k_x,\ y = 166317 + 166320\,k_y,\ k_x \ge 1,\ k_y \ge 0\},$$
with $j = y/3 = 55439 + 55440\,k_y$, $w = 3^j$, and for any putative cube root $z$ we set
$A = z - w$.

### Definition 3.1 (Branch field and the 3-adic cube-root branch)
For $(x,y) \in \mathcal L$, let $N(x) := 2^x + 5 \in \mathbb Z$. Since $N(x) \equiv 37 \equiv
1 \pmod 9$ and $37$ is a cube mod $3^n$ for all $n$ (problem.md Prop.), there is a unique
$\beta(x) \in \mathbb Z_3$ with $\beta(x)^3 = N(x)$ and $\beta(x) \equiv 1 \pmod 3$ (Hensel;
the $z \equiv 1$ branch). Call $\beta(x)$ the **principal 3-adic cube root** of $N(x)$.
(Note $A = z - w$ and $z^3 - w^3 = N(x)$, so $A$ is an integer approximation to a root of
$T^3 + 3wT^2 + 3w^2T - N(x)$; modulo any power of 3, $w \equiv 0$ and $A \equiv z \equiv
\beta(x)$.)

### Definition 3.2 (Moving 3-adic depth and the depth filtration $\mathcal F$)
For an integer $a$ coprime to $3$ define the **depth of $a$ to the moving target** by
$$\delta_x(a) := v_3\big(N(x) - a^3\big) = v_3\big((a - \beta(x))(a^2 + a\beta(x) + \beta(x)^2)\big).$$
The **depth filtration** is $\mathcal F = \{F_n(x)\}_{n \ge 0}$ with
$F_n(x) := \{a \in \mathbb Z : 3 \nmid a,\ \delta_x(a) \ge n\}$. Each $F_n(x)$ is the
preimage of the ball $B(\beta(x), 3^{-?})$ under the cubing map and is a union of residue
classes mod $3^{\lceil n/1 \rceil}$ (because $a^2 + a\beta + \beta^2$ is a 3-adic unit when
$a \equiv \beta \pmod 3$). **Key fact (Theorem N restated):** a genuine Theorem-5 solution
has $A \in F_{j+1}(x) \setminus F_{j+2}(x)$, i.e. **exactly depth $j+1$.**

### Definition 3.3 (Archimedean size class)
For $(x,y) \in \mathcal L$ let $S(x) := 2^{x/3}$ (the real cube-root scale). Define the
**size class** of an integer $a > 0$ as $\sigma_x(a) := \log a / \log S(x) = 3\log a / (x\log
2) \in \mathbb R_{>0}$. The Session-4 bounds give, for a genuine $A$,
$\sigma_x(A) \in [\,1 - \varepsilon_x,\ 1 + \varepsilon_x\,]$ with $\varepsilon_x \to 0$
(precisely $2^{x/3}/3 \le A \le 1.587\cdot 2^{x/3}$, so $\sigma_x(A) \to 1$).

### Definition 3.4 (The Coupling Height $\widehat H$)
For $(x,y) \in \mathcal L$ and an integer $a$ coprime to 3 with $a > 0$, define the
**coupling height**
$$\widehat H_x(a) := \sigma_x(a) \cdot \big(1 + \delta_x(a)\big) \cdot \mathbf 1[\,a^2+a\,3^j+3^{2j} \text{ has all prime factors} \equiv 1 \bmod 3\,] ,$$
i.e. the *product* (not sum) of the archimedean size class and the 3-adic depth, gated by
the Eisenstein-norm condition on $B = a^2 + a\,3^j + 3^{2j}$ (which must hold for a genuine
solution, since $B$ is an Eisenstein norm). **This is non-additive across the places
$\{\infty, 3\}$ by construction.** The gate indicator is the "place-2-and-up" content.

### Definition 3.5 (Progression-Rigidity Functional $\rho$)
Define $\rho : \mathcal L \to \mathbb R_{\ge 0}$ by
$$\rho(x,y) := \big|\,x \log 2 - (2j+1)\log 3\,\big| \;=\; |D|, \qquad D = x\log 2 - (2j+1)\log 3,$$
the **log-distance to the two-S-units boundary**. Sessions 14–15 established
$D = 110880\log 3 \cdot (1.8928\,k_x - 1.107 - k_y)/\log 3$-type linear form; the surviving
("$D \ge 0$") cases have $\rho$ bounded below by $\approx 55$ over $k_x \le 5000$ (Wolfram,
Session 15). The functional $\rho$ is the **archimedean coupling partner** of the 3-adic
depth $\delta$: small $\widehat H$ forces small $A$ forces small $D$ forces small $\rho$.

### Definition 3.6 (The Phantom valuation and the separation invariant)
Set $\Phi := (5, -3, 10/3)$, the phantom, with $A_\Phi = 3$, $\delta(A_\Phi) = v_3(37 -
27) = v_3(10) = 0$ (depth 0, since $A_\Phi = 3 \equiv 0 \bmod 3$ is *excluded* from the
domain of $\delta$ — the phantom's $A$ is not 3-coprime). Define the **separation invariant**
$$\mathrm{Sep}(x,y) := \delta_x(A) - \delta(A_\Phi) = (j+1) - 0 = j + 1 \quad\text{for a genuine solution.}$$
The phantom lies *outside* the domain of the filtration (it has $v_3(A_\Phi) = 1$), and every
genuine solution lies at depth $j + 1 \to \infty$. **This is the formal statement that the
coupling height separates genuine solutions from the phantom**, the one separation the
standard height cannot see.

## §4. SUB-STEP 4 — Problem Translation & Testing

### 4.1 Translation of the Kuromine equation

A Theorem-5 integer solution $(x, y, z)$ with $z = w + A$ is, in the new language, **exactly
an integer $A > 0$, coprime to 3, with $(x,y) \in \mathcal L$, satisfying simultaneously:**

- **(T-real)** $\sigma_x(A) \in [1-\varepsilon_x, 1+\varepsilon_x]$ — the archimedean size law
  (Def 3.3), forcing $\rho(x,y) = |D|$ small *only if* $A$ is near the boundary scale;
- **(T-adic)** $A \in F_{j+1}(x) \setminus F_{j+2}(x)$ — depth *exactly* $j+1$ (Def 3.2 /
  Theorem N);
- **(T-gate)** $B = A^2 + A\,3^j + 3^{2j}$ has all prime factors $\equiv 1 \bmod 3$ (Eisenstein
  norm gate, Def 3.4);
- **(T-couple)** the *same* $x$ controls both the real scale $S(x) = 2^{x/3}$ and the depth
  bound $j + 1 = 55440 + 55440 k_y$ via the rigid lattice $\mathcal L$.

The conjecture "no Theorem-5 solution exists" becomes:

> **Coupling-Height Vanishing Conjecture.** For every $(x,y) \in \mathcal L$, no integer
> $A$ realizes (T-real)∧(T-adic)∧(T-gate) simultaneously; equivalently, the coupling height
> $\widehat H_x$ admits **no** integer point at the genuine locus
> $\{\sigma_x \approx 1,\ \delta_x = j+1\}$.

### 4.2 The two Lemmas a future session must prove

The contradiction is to be forced by showing the *real* constraint (T-real) and the
*3-adic depth* constraint (T-adic) are **jointly unsatisfiable along the lattice $\mathcal
L$** — precisely the cross-place coupling that no existing tool measures. Two lemmas would
suffice.

---

**Lemma A (Depth–Size Incompatibility).**
*There is an effective constant $c_0 > 0$ such that for every $(x,y) \in \mathcal L$ with
$k_x \ge 1$, every integer $a$ with $\delta_x(a) \ge j + 1$ (depth at least the genuine
depth) satisfies the archimedean lower bound*
$$\big|\,\sigma_x(a) - 1\,\big| \;\ge\; c_0 \cdot \frac{j+1}{x} \;=\; c_0 \cdot \frac{\delta_x(a)}{x}.$$
*In words: forcing an integer to be 3-adically close to $\beta(x)$ to depth $j+1$ forces it
archimedean-far from the genuine size scale $S(x)$ by an amount proportional to the depth.*

*Why it resolves the problem (if true):* A genuine solution needs **both** $\delta_x(A) =
j+1$ (T-adic) **and** $\sigma_x(A) \to 1$, i.e. $|\sigma_x(A) - 1| = O(\varepsilon_x) =
o(1/x \cdot \log\text{const})$ (T-real, Def 3.3 gives $|\sigma_x(A)-1| \le \log(1.587)/(\,
\tfrac{x}{3}\log2\,) = O(1/x)$). Lemma A would give $|\sigma_x(A) - 1| \ge c_0(j+1)/x$. Since
$j + 1 = 55440(k_y + 1) \to \infty$ while the genuine band has $|\sigma_x(A)-1| = O(1/x)$
with $x = 5 + 332640k_x$, comparing: Lemma A forces $|\sigma_x(A)-1| \gtrsim c_0\cdot
55440(k_y+1)/x$, whereas T-real caps it at $\approx 1.38/x$ (from $\log 1.587 \approx 0.462$,
times $3/\log 2$). For $k_y \ge 1$ (or any $k_y$ once $c_0\cdot55440 > 1.38$, which holds for
any $c_0 > 2.5\times10^{-5}$) these are **incompatible** — contradiction. This is the
**coupling**: it plays the large 3-adic depth $j+1$ directly against the tight real size
window, which is exactly the genuine-vs-phantom distinction (the phantom has depth $0$, so
it escapes — correctly, since it is not an integer solution).

*Honest status:* Lemma A is the crux and is **not proven**. It asserts a quantitative
archimedean *repulsion* caused by deep 3-adic approximation — a genuinely new type of
statement (a "3-adic-depth ⟹ real-distance" transfer). It does **not** follow from any
known measure (those compare two reals or two 3-adics, never a 3-adic depth against a real
size). Whether such a transfer can hold is open; if the cubing map's 3-adic and archimedean
geometries are "independent enough," it is plausible, but it could also be false (the
phantom shows depth and size can decouple at depth 0). The lemma must therefore quantify
*why depth $\ge j+1$, specifically, repels*, presumably using that $\beta(x)$ is an
algebraic-over-$\mathbb Q$ irrational reduction and $A$ is a rational integer.

---

**Lemma B (Lattice Rigidity / No Simultaneous Approximant).**
*Let $D(x,y) = x\log 2 - (2j+1)\log 3 = \rho(x,y)$ on the surviving wedge $0 \le D$,
$k_y/k_x < 0.893$. Then there is an effective $c_1 > 0$ such that for all $(x,y)\in\mathcal
L$ with $k_x \ge 1$,*
$$\rho(x,y) = |D(x,y)| \;\ge\; c_1 \cdot (j+1)\,\big/\,\log(j+1),$$
*i.e. the Theorem-5 lattice never approaches the two-S-units boundary $D = 0$ faster than
linearly in the depth (up to a log).*

*Why it resolves the problem (if true):* Combine with the size law. The genuine size window
$\sigma_x(A)\to 1$ holds iff $A \approx 2^{x/3}/3^{?}$ near the boundary, and the
factorization size bound $A < (2^x+5)/3^{2j} \approx 3e^{D}$ (Theorem N scope note) ties
$A$'s size to $e^{D} = e^{\rho}$. A genuine solution with depth $j+1$ needs $A$ in the
depth-$(j+1)$ ball **and** of size $\approx e^{\rho}$. Lemma B says $\rho \gtrsim (j+1)/\log(j+1)$,
so $A \gtrsim e^{(j+1)/\log(j+1)}$, an enormous size; but the depth-$(j+1)$ ball $F_{j+1}(x)$
has its smallest positive integer representative of size $\gtrsim 3^{?}$ tied to $\beta(x)
\bmod 3^{j+1}$, and Lemma A would force that representative *off* the genuine size band.
Lemmas A and B **together** close the wedge: A handles $k_y \ge 1$ (depth grows), B confines
the only remaining strip ($k_y = 0$, the family floor) by bounding $\rho$ from below so the
size band and depth ball cannot intersect. (Session 15 already verified numerically that the
*closest surviving* approach has $D \approx 55$ — consistent with, and a special case of,
Lemma B.)

*Honest status:* Lemma B is a **linear-form-in-two-logs lower bound with a depth-dependent
right-hand side**. A *fixed* lower bound $\rho \ge c$ is classical (Baker: $|x\log2 -
(2j+1)\log3| \gg \max(x,j)^{-C}$), but that decays in $j$, the **wrong** direction. Lemma B
demands $\rho$ *grow* with depth $j$ — which is false in general for linear forms (they get
*smaller* as heights grow, by Dirichlet). So **Lemma B as stated is too strong and likely
false in isolation**; it must be *relativized to the lattice $\mathcal L$* (the progression
forces $D$ to specific quantized values $D = 110880\log3\cdot(\text{integer offset}) + \ldots$,
so $D$ cannot be arbitrarily small — it lives on a 1-dimensional lattice of spacing
$\approx 110880\log 3 \approx 121800$). The correct form of Lemma B is therefore:
> **(B′)** On $\mathcal L$, $D$ takes values in a coset of the rank-1 lattice
> $110880\log3 \cdot \mathbb Z + (5\log2 - (2\cdot55439+1)\log3)$; the *minimal nonnegative*
> value over $k_x \le K$ is bounded below by an effective $c_1 > 0$ independent of depth
> *because the spacing $\approx 121800$ dwarfs the residual*.
This relativized (B′) is *plausible and partially verified* (Session 15's $D\approx55$ floor),
and is the realistic target. It replaces the false "grows with depth" claim by "is bounded
below by lattice spacing," which is all Lemma A needs to fire for $k_y = 0$.
