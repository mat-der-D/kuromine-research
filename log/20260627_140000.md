### B. New Wolfram computation — the 5-adic valuation portrait of $N = 2^x+3^y+5$

Setup (Theorem-5 family): $x = 5 + 332640\,k_x$, $y = 166317 + 166320\,k_y$, $N = z^3$.

**(B1) Cube map at 5 is a bijection (no residue obstruction).** $\gcd(3,|(\mathbb Z/5^n)^*|)=
\gcd(3,4\cdot5^{n-1})=1$, so cubes mod $5^n$ are *all* residues (Wolfram: cubes mod 5 $=\{0,1,2,3,4\}$).
Thus a *residue* condition at 5 is empty — consistent with the universal soft obstruction. The
content is purely in the **valuation**.

**(B2) $v_5(N) \geq 2$ universally (Wolfram).** $\operatorname{ord}_{25}(2)=\operatorname{ord}_{25}
(3)=20$ and $332640\equiv166320\equiv0\pmod{20}$, so $2^x\equiv7$, $3^y\equiv13\pmod{25}$ are pinned
across the family; $N\equiv7+13+5=25\equiv0\pmod{25}$. (Also $x\equiv1\pmod4$, $y\equiv1\pmod4$
constant $\Rightarrow 2^x+3^y\equiv2+3\equiv0\pmod5$, giving $v_5\geq1$ directly; mod 25 lifts it to
$\geq2$.)

**(B3) First-level cube condition (Wolfram).** A cube needs $3\mid v_5(N)$; with $v_5\geq2$ this
forces $v_5\geq3$, i.e. $N\equiv0\pmod{125}$. With $\operatorname{ord}_{125}(2)=\operatorname{ord}_{125}
(3)=100$, $332640\equiv40$, $166320\equiv20\pmod{100}$ (both period 5 in their parameters), the
$5\times5$ grid of $(k_x,k_y)\bmod5$ gives $N\equiv0\pmod{125}$ on **exactly the diagonal**
$$\{(0,4),(1,0),(2,1),(3,2),(4,3)\} = \{\,k_y \equiv k_x-1 \pmod 5\,\}.$$
So **80% of the family ($v_5(N)=2$) is not a cube and is eliminated**; survivors satisfy
$k_y\equiv k_x-1\pmod5$.

**(B4) Iterated sieve (Wolfram, $25\times25$ block, $v_5$ read at modulus $5^{40}$).** On the
survival diagonal the $v_5$ distribution over 125 points is $\{3{:}100,\ 4{:}20,\ 5{:}4,\ 6{:}1\}$.
Points with $v_5\in\{4,5\}$ (not $\equiv0\pmod3$) are eliminated; survivors $v_5\in\{3,6\}$ number
$101/125$, i.e. $\approx16.2\%$ of the full mod-25 grid. The cut continues 5-adically.

**(B5) Soft-obstruction / phantom check (Wolfram).** Phantom $1000/27=(10/3)^3$: $v_5=v_5(1000)=3
\equiv0\pmod3$ — passes. Its parameters $k_x=0$, $k_y=-1$ (from $y=-3$) satisfy $k_y-(k_x-1)=0
\pmod5$: **on the survival diagonal.** Random sample of 4000 $(k_x,k_y)\in[0,5^4)^2$: survivor
fraction $16.7\%$, bounded away from 0. The sieve removes a fixed positive fraction at every depth
and **never empties the family**.

**(B6) No known solution removed (Wolfram).** $(1,0,2)$: $N=8$, $v_5=0$ (cube). $(5,3,4)$: $N=64$,
$v_5=0$ (cube). Both lie outside the Theorem-5 family ($x<6$ / $y<2$), so the family-only $v_5\geq2$
fact does not touch them.
