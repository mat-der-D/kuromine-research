## Work Done (all Wolfram MCP)

1. **Re-derived the three frame-constants to 30 digits** and verified the clean identity
   $3 - 3\log_3 2 = 3\log_3(3/2) = 1.10721073928562768870\ldots$ (also
   $2\log3/(3\log2)=1.05664\ldots$, $3\log2/\log3=1.89279\ldots$). Matches the record.

2. **Computed the realized gap exponent vs $z^3$** for surviving Theorem-5 pairs:
   $E(k_x,k_y) = \log(2^x+5)/\log(z^3)$ where $x=5+332640k_x$, $j=55439+55440k_y$, $y=3j$,
   $z^3 = 2^x+3^y+5$ (using the survival condition $3^{2j+1}\le 2^x+4$, Theorem M). Result
   over $k_x \le 10$ and all surviving $k_y$:
   - At $k_y=0$ ($2^x \gg 3^y$): $E \to 1^-$. For $k_x=1$, $\log(3^y)-\log(2^x) = -47854.04$
     exactly (the classic Baker near-coincidence), so $E = 1 - e^{-47854}/\log z^3$ — strictly
     below 1 but by an astronomically tiny amount.
   - At the survival boundary ($2^x \approx 3^{2j+1}$): $E \to 2/3$ (derived analytically:
     on the boundary $z^3 = u^3 + 3u^2 + 5$ with $u=3^j$, gap $= 3u^2+5 \sim (z^3)^{2/3}$).
   - **Over the entire surviving family, $E \in (2/3, 1)$, strictly $< 1$.**

3. **Computed the gap exponent vs $3^y$** ($\log(2^x+5)/\log(3^y)$) for contrast: ranges from
   $\sim 0.67$ at the boundary up to $\sim 10$ at $k_y=0$ (where $3^y$ is comparatively small).
   This wide-ranging normalization is the one in which "$1.107$" was implicitly mixed.

4. **Located the actual hard-boundary regime.** At the largest surviving $k_y$ for each
   $k_x \ge 2$, the log-ratio $\log(3^y)/\log(z^2) = 1.5$ **exactly** — i.e. $3^y \approx z^3$,
   not $3^y \approx z^2$. The "$z^2$" of older logs comes only from the auxiliary
   $2^{2/3}$-Thue casting ($z^3 - 4W^3 = 3^y+5$, $W=2^a$), not from the direct $z^3-3^y$ gap.

## Results

1. **Corrected open-target framing (the session's substantive output).** The realized
   exponent of $|z^3-3^y| = 2^x+5$ measured against $z^3$ is **strictly below 1 across the
   whole surviving Theorem-5 family** ($E \in (2/3,1)$). Therefore the necessary-and-sufficient
   open target is:
   > An unconditional **effective** lower bound $|z^3-3^y| > C\,(z^3)^{\kappa}$ with **any
   > fixed $\kappa>1$** resolves the entire surviving family.

   The long-recorded "$\kappa < 1.107$ (vs $z^3$)" is a **sufficient margin**
   ($1.107 = 3-3\log_3 2$), not a necessary ceiling. $\kappa=1$ exactly is *just barely*
   insufficient because $E\to1^-$ at $k_y=0$ (deficit $\sim e^{-47854}$), so the requirement
   is strict: $\kappa>1$.

2. **The substantive impasse is unchanged.** The available Baker-type machinery yields
   $v_p(\Lambda_p)=0$ (Session 5), which is exactly the statement that no positive effective
   $\kappa$ gap bound exists — the realized exponent sitting at $1-e^{-47854}$ is the
   two-comparable-S-units coincidence itself. "Available" ($\kappa$ effectively $\le 1$, in
   fact no positive effective gap bound) vs "needed" ($\kappa>1$) remains a genuine gap.

3. **Two prior framing imprecisions corrected for the record:** (i) the threshold is a
   *sufficient* value, not the true ceiling (true requirement $\kappa>1$); (ii) the hard
   boundary is $3^y\approx z^3$, not $3^y\approx z^2$ (the latter is an artifact of the
   $2^{2/3}$-Thue auxiliary casting).

## Conclusion

This session produced a genuine, Wolfram-verified **sharpening and correction** of how the
single open target is stated, without changing the mathematical state of the problem. The
target is cleaner than recorded — "any effective $\kappa>1$" rather than the mis-normalized
"$\kappa<1.107$ vs $z^3$ in the $3^y\approx z^2$ regime" — but it remains exactly as far out
of reach: no effective gap bound with $\kappa>1$ exists for the $3^y\approx 2^x\approx z^3$
two-comparable-S-units regime, because Baker's linear forms give $v_p(\Lambda_p)=0$. The
correction is important for future WATCH triage: a result advertising "effective $\kappa>1$
separation of cubes from powers of 3 in the comparable regime" is now correctly flagged as
*decisive*, whereas the old "$<1.107$" wording could mislead a future session into discarding
a sufficient ($\kappa$ slightly above 1) result.
