## Work Done

### 1. Size landscape (Wolfram)
For the smallest admissible Theorem-5 parameters $x = 332645$, $y = 166317$:
- $x\log2 \approx 230572$, $y\log3 \approx 182718$.
- $2^x/3^y \approx e^{47854}$ — the $2^x$ term dominates $3^y$ enormously.
- $z \approx 2^{x/3}$, $w = 3^{y/3}$, and $z/w \approx e^{15951}$.

**Implication:** the factorization $(z-w)(z^2+zw+w^2) = 2^x+5$ is maximally *unbalanced*
($z-w\approx z$, $z^2+zw+w^2\approx z^2$, both large). The Session-1 "small factor" hope
is void. The clean reframing is $z^3 - 2^x = 3^y+5$: a cube very close to a power of 2.

### 2. 2-adic structure (Wolfram)
- Computed $v_2(3^y+5) = 3$ for $y = 166317 + 166320m$, $m=0,\dots,6$ — **constant** on
  the whole family (because $y$ is pinned mod a small power of 2).
- Since $x \ge 332645 \gg 3$: $v_2(z^3) = v_2(3^y+5) = 3$, so $v_2(z) = 1$ exactly;
  $z \equiv 2 \pmod 4$.
- The cube map is a bijection on $(\mathbb{Z}/2^n)^\*$ (since $\gcd(3,2^{n-1})=1$), so
  $z$ is *uniquely determined* mod $2^x$ by $3^y+5$. Writing $z=2u$, solving
  $u^3 \equiv (3^y+5)/8 \pmod{2^{x-3}}$ gives $u\equiv3\pmod{16}$, hence
  $z \equiv 6 \pmod{32}$ (sharper than Session-1's $z\equiv6\pmod8$).

### 3. The soft-obstruction principle (proof)
The phantom value is $2^5 + 3^{-3} + 5 = 1000/27 = (10/3)^3$ — a **perfect cube in
$\mathbb{Q}$**. Therefore it is a cube modulo every prime $p\ne3$ (reduce $10/3$), and at
$p=3$ the constrained value $37$ is a cube mod $3^n$ for all $n$ (Session 1). Combined
with cube-map bijectivity mod $2^n$ and mod $3^n$, the equation is **locally solvable at
every prime**. Hence no congruence / $p$-adic argument can, on its own, refute a
solution. This upgrades Session 1's observation ("phantom survives mod $m$") to a
structural theorem about *why*.

### 4. Literature check (WebSearch)
Confirmed the relevant machinery: Pillai's problem, Matveev's lower bound, Bennett's
rational-approximation-of-small-height results, and the 2025 paper
"Arithmetic holonomy bounds and effective Diophantine approximation" (arXiv:2510.04156),
which sharpens effective irrationality measures — the kind of global tool needed here.

### 5. Brute-force sanity check (Wolfram)

matching the known solutions and the framework's consistency.
