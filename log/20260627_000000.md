### 3. Parity structure of $c_m$

**Claim:** $c_m$ is odd iff $m$ is odd.

**Proof:** $c_m \equiv m^3 + m^2 + m \equiv m(m^2+m+1) \pmod 2$. For $m$ even: $c_m \equiv 0$
(even). For $m$ odd: $m^2+m = m(m+1) \equiv 0 \pmod 2$ (product of consecutive integers),
so $m^2+m+1 \equiv 1$, hence $c_m \equiv m \equiv 1 \pmod 2$ (odd). $\square$

**Consequence for the equation $2^x + 3^y + c_m = z^3$** (for $x,y \geq 1$):

- $m$ odd (e.g., $m=1,3$): $c_m$ is odd, so $z^3 = 2^x + 3^y + c_m \equiv 0 + 1 + 1 \equiv 0
  \pmod 2$, hence **$z$ is even**. (Same as $c_1=5$.)
- $m$ even (e.g., $m=2,4$): $c_m$ is even, so $z^3 \equiv 3^y \equiv 1 \pmod 2$, hence
  **$z$ is odd**. (Different: 2-adic cube bijection acts on odd integers.)

### 6. Soft obstruction is universal across the family

**Claim:** The phantom is a global rational cube, hence $z_m$ is a cube in $\mathbb{Q}_p$ for
every prime $p \neq 3$.

**Proof:** $(9m+1)/3 \in \mathbb{Q}$ and $((9m+1)/3)^3 = z_m^3 = 2^5 + 3^{-3} + c_m$.

is a cube in $\mathbb{Q}_p$ for every $p \neq 3$. Hence the equation is locally solvable at
every prime $p \neq 3$. $\square$
