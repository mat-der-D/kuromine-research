## Results

- **New rigorous result:** In the surviving case, $y = v_3(z-w)+1$ where $w=(2^x+5)^{1/3}\in
  \mathbb{Z}_3$, with the cofactor $z^2+zw+w^2$ pinned to valuation exactly 1. This is elementary
  and certain.
- **Negative/limiting result:** This identity does **not** bound $y$. 3-adically $z-w$ can be
  arbitrarily small (the phantom is the explicit witness at valuation $-1$ in $z$), so the
  congruence-level obstruction genuinely persists — confirming, from a new angle, the dead-end
  status of pure sieving.
- **Reframing:** The case is equivalent to a 3-adic Diophantine-approximation statement
  ($z$ integer, 3-adically close to algebraic $w$), i.e. the natural input to Yu's p-adic
  linear forms in logarithms.

## Conclusion

The p-adic structure is now clean and explicit. The phantom is not a mysterious coincidence:
it is the unique non-integer point on the same 3-adic cube-root branch as the integer solution,
and the identity $y=v_3(z-w)+1$ makes the separation between "integer $z$" and "phantom $z$"
precise. Congruences cannot exploit this (they only test 3-adic integers); an *analytic*
effective bound (Baker/Yu) can. This both consolidates the dead-end reasoning and sharpens the
target for the next step.
