### C. THEORY-BUILDING — the Geometry-of-Numbers / Successive-Minima Framework

Full statement in `knowledge/references/geometry_of_numbers_framework.md`. The four mandated
sub-steps:

1. **Obstruction diagnosis (afresh).** Keep the problem 2-dimensional: a genuine solution is the
   simultaneous exact vanishing of two linear forms in logs,
   $L_1 = x\log2 - 3\log z = \log(1-(3^y+5)/z^3)$ and $L_2 = y\log3 - 3\log z = \log(1-(2^x+5)/z^3)$.
   These span a rank-2 log-lattice; the question is whether its successive minima admit a Minkowski
   determinant lower bound.

2. **Blueprint.** If both forms were simultaneously small with a non-degenerate Gram determinant,
   Minkowski's second theorem would give $\lambda_1\lambda_2\asymp\det$ and a lower bound on $\det$
   (a *pair*-transcendence statement) would force $\lambda_1$ up — a genuinely 2-D constraint no
   single cubic measure addresses.

3. **Axiomatic definition.** Log-form vector $\mathbf L=(L_1,L_2)$; eccentricity
   $\varepsilon=|L_2|/|L_1|$; minima proxies $\lambda_1=\min,\lambda_2=\max$; proxy determinant
   $\det=\lambda_1\lambda_2$.

4. **Translation + test lemmas (Wolfram-verified).**
   - **Lemma GN-1 (Degeneration Law) — PROVED, KEPT (genuine new fact).** On the survival wedge
     ($D\ge0$, $2^x\gtrsim3^y$): $|L_1|\approx(3^y+5)/2^x = e^{-(x\log2-y\log3)}$ is
     **super-exponentially small** (an $\sim10^5$-digit-exponent quantity already at $k_x=1$;
     Wolfram returned $L_1\approx0$ to $10^{-58}$, hitting the precision limit, confirming the size),
     while $|L_2|\approx x\log2-y\log3 = \Theta(D+j\log3)$ is **huge** ($\sim10^4$–$10^5$). Hence
     $\varepsilon\to\infty$ and $\det=\lambda_1\lambda_2\approx(x\log2-y\log3)e^{-(x\log2-y\log3)}\to0$:
     the log-lattice is **maximally degenerate**. **Exact offset identity (Wolfram, 8 digits):**
     $(-\log|L_1|)-D=(1-j)\log3$, verified as the AP $\{-60904.868,-121811.93,-182719.00\}$ for
     $k_y=0,1,2$, step $-55440\log3=-60907.065$. On the known solution $(5,3,4)$: $L_1=-0.6931$,
     $L_2=-0.8630$, $\varepsilon\approx1.25$ — **non-degenerate**, exactly as it must be (small
     genuine solutions are the well-conditioned points, already found).
   - **Lemma GN-2 (Determinant-Driven Exclusion, the crux) — REFUTED as a route.** Because
     $\lambda_2$ carries no smallness, the geometry-of-numbers problem **collapses to the single
     first minimum** $\lambda_1=|L_1|$, with $-\log\lambda_1 = x\log2-y\log3$ — the $\{\log2,\log3\}$
     linear-form gap. An effective lower bound on it strong enough to exclude the cube is precisely
