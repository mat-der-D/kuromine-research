### C. New Wolfram experiment — the realized gap exponent as a *range* statement

Computed (Wolfram, 40-digit) the realized gap exponent $E=\log(2^x+5)/\log(z^3)$ at the
**largest surviving** $k_y$ (i.e. $D\ge0$ but minimal) for several $k_x$, with
$x=5+332640\,k_x$, $y=166317+166320\,k_y$, $z^3=2^x+3^y+5$:

| $k_x$ | $k_y$ | $D=x\log2-(2j+1)\log3$ | $E=\log(2^x+5)/\log(z^3)$ |
|------|------|------|------|
| 1     | 0      | $108759$ | $1$ (i.e. $1-e^{-47854}$, the classic floor near-coincidence) |
| 2     | 2      | $95699$  | $0.84125105\ldots$ |
| 3     | 4      | $82639$  | $0.75712223\ldots$ |
| 1054  | 1993   | $121804$ | $0.66700098\ldots$ |
| 4729  | 8950   | $54.99$  | $0.66666670\ldots$ |
| 84180 | 159334 | $0.5367$ | $0.66666666672\ldots$ |

As the surviving pairs approach the two-comparable-S-units boundary $D\to0^+$, $E$ **decreases
monotonically to $2/3^+$**; at the family floor $k_y=0$, $E=1^-$. Combined with the S21
equidistribution fact $\inf_{k_x}D_{\ge0}=0$ (since $P/Q=3\log2/\log3$ is irrational), the
surviving pairs come arbitrarily close to the boundary, so $E$ **densely fills the open interval
$(2/3,1)$**, attaining neither endpoint but approaching both arbitrarily closely.

**Symbolic confirmation of the $2/3$ floor (Wolfram).** At the boundary $2^x\approx3^{2j+1}$ one
has $3^y=3^{3j}$ dominating $z^3$, so $\log z^3\approx 3j\log3$, while $\log(2^x+5)\approx
(2j+1)\log3$. Hence
$$E\to\lim_{j\to\infty}\frac{(2j+1)\log3}{3j\log3}=\lim_{j\to\infty}\frac{2j+1}{3j}=\frac23$$
(Wolfram `Limit` $=2/3$). Equivalently, at the boundary $2^x+5\approx z^2$ — the "$z^2$" that
appears in the auxiliary $2^{2/3}$-Thue casting (S3) — so $E\to\log(z^2)/\log(z^3)=2/3$.
