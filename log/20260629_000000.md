**New paper found (not previously assessed): arXiv:2510.00759** —
*"Proof by Mechanization: Cubic Diophantine Equation Satisfiability is Σ⁰₁-Complete"*
(also titled "Cubic incompleteness: Hilbert's tenth problem begins at degree three"), Feb 2026.
**Result:** satisfiability of a *single* Diophantine equation of **total degree ≤ 3** over $\mathbb N$
(number of variables encoded in the input) is **Σ⁰₁-complete**, hence undecidable. Mechanized
proof: a primitive-recursive compiler maps arithmetic-sentence codes to cubic constraint
systems; degree control is local (all checks compile to degree ≤ 2; the only degree-3 terms
arise when a linear selector activates a quadratic obligation). Undecidability by
diagonalization.

**Assessment (decisive, and it CONFIRMS the barrier).** This paper bears directly on the **S22
decidability/degree attack face**. The S22 face recorded that the KLNOW / Dong–Shafrir
decidability results (arXiv:2505.19141) settle linear-exponential systems only for **≤2 prime
bases and degree-≤1 free terms**, and that the Kuromine free term — the cube $z^3$ — is degree 3
and outside that class; the open hope was a *future extension* of decidability to degree-≥2 /
degree-3 free terms.

arXiv:2510.00759 shows that hope is **impossible in general**: degree 3 is exactly where
*universal* undecidability begins (Hilbert's tenth problem "begins at degree three"). So a
*blanket* decidability extension to degree-3 free terms **cannot exist** — any decidability
result reaching Kuromine would have to exploit Kuromine's *special* structure (two fixed-base
exponentials plus a single cube), not the general degree-3 class. This is a **negative**
signal for the decidability route: it converts the S22 "currently out of class" into "the
general class is provably undecidable," i.e. the route can only ever be a bespoke
special-structure argument, never a general decision procedure.

Conversely — and stated honestly — undecidability of the *general* degree-3 class does **NOT**
imply Kuromine itself is undecidable: Kuromine is a single, highly structured family, not the
universal cubic class. So 2510.00759 neither resolves nor refutes the Kuromine conjecture; it
**sharpens why** the decidability face is hard (degree 3 = the universal-undecidability
threshold) and removes "wait for a general degree-3 decidability theorem" as a viable WATCH
target.
