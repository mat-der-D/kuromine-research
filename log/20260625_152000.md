## Work Done

### 1. The $A\equiv3\pmod p$ phenomenon (Wolfram)
Observed that $332640\equiv0\pmod3$ and $166320\equiv0\pmod6$, so $x\bmod3\equiv2$ and
$y\bmod6\equiv3$ are **constant** over the entire Theorem-5 family. Hence for any prime $p$
with $\operatorname{ord}_p(2)\mid332640$ and $\operatorname{ord}_p(3)\mid55440,166320$, the
values $2^x,3^y,w=3^j\pmod p$ are pinned. Computed $A=z-w\pmod p$ for primes $p\equiv2\pmod3$
($p=11,17,23,29,41,71,89,113,281$) and found **$A\equiv3\pmod p$ in every case**; for
$p\equiv1\pmod3$ the phantom-matching cube-root branch gives $A\equiv3$, $B\equiv37/3$.

### 2. Identified the cause (Wolfram)
The phantom $(5,-3,10/3)$ has $A_{\text{phantom}} = \tfrac{10}{3}-3^{-1}=3$ and
$B_{\text{phantom}} = \tfrac{37}{3}$, with $A_{\text{phantom}}B_{\text{phantom}}=37=2^5+5$.
Since the family's $(z,w)$ reduces to the phantom's $(10/3,1/3)$ mod every prime $p\ne3$, we
get $A\equiv3$, $B\equiv37/3\pmod p$ automatically. **This is the soft-obstruction principle
on the factorization:** any congruence about $A$ or $B$ is satisfied by the phantom, so no
prime $p$ yields a contradiction. The naive primitive-divisor / congruence attack is dead.

### 3. Pinned the genuine real-vs-phantom distinction (Wolfram)
Integer solution: $v_3(A)=0$, $A\equiv1\pmod3$, $A\bmod9\in\{1,4,7\}$, $B$ a genuine integer
with $v_3(B)=0$. Phantom: $A=3$ ($v_3=1$, $\bmod9=3$), $B=37/3$ ($v_3=-1$). They differ only
at powers of 3 (where the soft obstruction does not apply), but the difference is
non-actionable: $z^3\equiv2^x+5\equiv1\pmod9$ has self-consistent roots $z\equiv1,4,7\pmod9$,
and $37$ is a cubic residue mod $3^n$ for all $n$ — the known $3^n$ wall. Also noted $B$ is
**not** a Lucas/Lehmer ($a^n-b^n$) sequence, so Zsygmondy/BHV do not apply.

### 4. Confirmed $A$ is huge (Wolfram)
From $4B-A^2=3(z+w)^2$ and $AB=2^x+5$: $A^3<4(2^x+5)$ so $A<1.587\cdot2^{x/3}$, and
$B<3z^2$ gives $A>2^{x/3}/3$. Thus $A\sim2^{x/3}$ for any Theorem-5 solution — "$A=1$"
(true for both real solutions) is impossible there. Aspirational sub-question (c) closed.

### 5. Modular / Frey-curve assessment (WebSearch + reasoning)
Confirmed the modular method requires a *varying prime exponent* to carry the mod-$p$ Galois
representation (Darmon's program; Bennett–Skinner; Bugeaud–Mignotte–Siksek multi-Frey). The
Kuromine equation has only the fixed small cube exponent, with $x,y$ on fixed small bases —
no varying prime exponent — so no Frey curve can be built. The fixed-$y$ elliptic/Mordell
route only re-derives the known per-$y$ finiteness. Documented in
`knowledge/references/modular_method.md`.

### 6. Numerical verification (Python, cubic-residue sieve mod 14 primes)
