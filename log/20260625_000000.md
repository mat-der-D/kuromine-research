### 2. Baker's Method Assessment

**Setup:** For solutions with $x = 5 + 332640k$ and $y = 166317 + 166320m$ (Theorem 5 class), $2^x \gg 3^y$ at the minimum case ($x = 332645$, $y = 166317$):

$$\log(2^x) = 332645 \cdot \log 2 \approx 230572, \quad \log(3^y) = 166317 \cdot \log 3 \approx 182718$$

So $2^x / 3^y \approx e^{47854}$, meaning $z^3 \approx 2^x \gg 3^y$.

**Linear form:** $\Lambda = x \log 2 - 3 \log z \approx -(3^y + 5)/2^x$, so $\log|\Lambda| \approx -47854$.

**Matveev bound (n=2 terms):** $\log|\Lambda| > -C \cdot \log(2) \cdot \log(z) \cdot (1 + \log x)$ where $C = 1.4 \cdot 4^{4.5} \cdot 30^5 \approx 7.7 \times 10^8$.

**Result:** The Matveev bound gives $\log|\Lambda| > -5.6 \times 10^{14}$, which does not contradict $\log|\Lambda| \approx -47854$. **Baker's method (standard form) yields no contradiction** for these parameter values.

$$\boxed{v_3(z^3 - 37) = 4 + v_3(k)}$$

### 5. Factorization Approach (New)

**From the y-divisibility:** Setting $w = 3^{y/3}$, the equation $z^3 = 2^x + 3^y + 5$ becomes:

$$\boxed{(z - w)(z^2 + zw + w^2) = 2^x + 5}$$

**Coprimality proof:** $\gcd(z - w, z^2 + zw + w^2) = 1$.
- $z \equiv 1 \pmod 3$ (since $z^3 \equiv 2^x + 3^y + 5 \equiv 2 + 0 + 2 \equiv 1 \pmod 3$ with $x$ odd).
- $w \equiv 0 \pmod 3$. So both factors are $\equiv 1 \pmod 3$ and $\gcd$ must equal 1.

**Prime factor constraint:** All prime factors of $B = z^2 + zw + w^2$ are $\equiv 1 \pmod 3$.
- Proof: $p \mid B$ with $p \neq 3$ implies $(z/w)^2 + (z/w) + 1 \equiv 0 \pmod p$, requiring $-3$ to be a QR mod $p$, which holds iff $p \equiv 1 \pmod 3$.

**Norm form:** $B = N_{\mathbb{Z}[\omega]/\mathbb{Z}}(z - \omega w)$ in the Eisenstein integers. Since $2 \equiv 5 \equiv 2 \pmod 3$ (both inert in $\mathbb{Z}[\omega]$), the factorization of $2^x + 5$ in $\mathbb{Z}[\omega]$ has a constrained form.

**Parity analysis:**
- $z \equiv 6 \pmod 8$ (from $z^3 \equiv 24 \pmod{32}$, verified for $y \equiv 1 \pmod 4$, which holds since $166317 \equiv 1 \pmod 4$).
- $w \equiv 3 \pmod 8$ (since $q$ is odd: $27^q \equiv 3 \pmod 8$, so $9 \cdot 27^q \equiv 27 \equiv 3 \pmod 8$).
- $z - w \equiv 3 \pmod 8$, $z^2 + zw + w^2 \equiv 7 \pmod 8$.
- Product: $21 \equiv 5 \pmod 8 = 2^x + 5 \pmod 8$ for $x \geq 3$. $\checkmark$

**Connection to Thue-Mahler:** For fixed $k$ (hence fixed $N_k = 2^{5+332640k} + 5$), the equation $z^3 - w^3 = N_k$ with $w = 3^j$ is a Thue-Mahler equation. The Thue-Mahler theorem guarantees finitely many solutions for each fixed $N_k$. **Open question:** Can we show zero solutions for all $k \geq 1$?
