## Results

- **Main finding:** The phantom is *exactly* the $y=-3$ point of the equation's $x=5$ fibre.
  This makes precise *why* congruences fail (they cannot see the sign of $y$) and *why* Baker's
  method is the correct tool (the real branch $y\ge2$ separates from the phantom only at the
  archimedean place).
- **Negative (sharp):** The 3-adic admissible-$x$ density does not decay (~0.18 limit), so no
  refinement of the congruence/Theorem-5 sieve on $x$ can succeed. This closes a loophole one
  might have hoped to exploit.
- **Negative (search):** No solutions besides $(5,3,4)$ for $x\equiv5\pmod6,\ x\le600,\ y\le300$.
- **Structural:** The three-term equation cleanly reduces to two two-term linear forms; an
  absolute effective bound needs the archimedean *and* the 3-adic form combined.

insufficient because the phantom is a real 3-adic point. This is concrete and actionable.
