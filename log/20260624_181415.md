## Work done

**Setup.** Verified the known solutions $(1,0,2),(5,3,4)$ and the phantom $(5,-3,10/3)$.

**1. Phantom is two-sided.** Proved/verified symbolically:
- A unit $u$ is a cube mod $3^n$ for every $n$ iff $u\equiv\pm1\pmod9$ (checked $n=2..8$).
  Here $x\equiv5\pmod6\Rightarrow2^x+5\equiv1\pmod9$, so the 3-adic cube condition always
  holds (this is the classical "37 is a cube mod $3^n$" phantom).
- Cubing is a **bijection** on $(\mathbb Z/2^n)^\times$ (since $\gcd(3,2^{n-1})=1$; checked
  $n=3..12$). With $v_2(3^y+5)=3$ exactly in the open region, $3^y+5=8\cdot(\text{odd})$ is
  always a cube times $8$, so the 2-adic condition is automatic (forces only $z\equiv2\pmod4$).

**2. Baker via the cube root is ineffective.** The only linear form the cube condition forces
small involves $\log z$, whose height grows linearly in $x$, so Matveev/Laurent bounds are
too weak. Identified the variable cube base $z^3$ as the essential obstruction (contrast with
$a^x+b^y=c^z$ where all bases are fixed). Confirmed Baker would help only in a balanced
regime that the cube condition does not force.

**3. Factorization.** Since $3\mid166320$, the open region has $y\equiv0\pmod3$, so
$3^y=v^3$, $v=3^{y/3}$, and
$$(z-v)(z^2+zv+v^2)=2^x+5.$$
Showed $2^x+5$ is odd and $\not\equiv0\pmod3$, hence $\gcd(z-v,\,z^2+zv+v^2)=1$ — a coprime
factorization (same mechanism as Theorem 4).
- Subcase $A:=z-v=1$ gives $3v^2+3v-4=2^x\Rightarrow2^x\equiv23\pmod{27}\Rightarrow
  x\equiv11\pmod{18}$, contradicting $x\equiv5\pmod{18}$ ($18\mid332640$). **Eliminated.**
- For general $A$, mod $3^{s+1}$ reduces to $A^3\equiv2^x+5$, always solvable (phantom);
  the binding shadow is $A\equiv13\pmod{27}$.

**4. Divisor sieve.** Computed, by two independent methods, the set of primes that can divide
$2^x+5$ for some $x\equiv5\pmod{332640}$. Allowed small primes:
$37,47,53,59,103,139,167,173,179,\dots$; almost all small primes (5,7,11,13,...) are
forbidden. Density of allowed primes up to 5000 is $\approx0.12$.
