## Work Done

### 1. Badziahin "Distance between cubics and rationals" — statements (from search abstracts; arxiv.org/Springer return HTTP 403 to the fetch tool, as in prior sessions)

The paper studies the set D_{3,1} of pairs (u, v) of positive reals such that
|ξ − p/q| > c · H(ξ)^{−u} · q^{−v} holds for **all cubic irrationals ξ** and all
rationals p/q (H = naive height of ξ). It transforms the question into **root
separation of cubic polynomials** and proves:

- **Under abc:** for 2 ≤ v ≤ 3, the interior of D_{3,1} is characterised by **u > 10 − 3v**.
- **Under Hall's conjecture** (a special case of abc): every (u,v) = (3+2ε, 5/2+ε) lies in
  D_{3,1}, and every (u,v) ∈ D_{3,1} satisfies u ≥ 10 − 3v.
- **All results are conditional** (on abc or Hall). No unconditional separation improvement.

Crucially, the bounds are only nontrivial in the **good-approximation regime 2 ≤ v ≤ 3**:
they describe how close a *good* rational approximation to a cubic irrational can be.

### 2. Can Kuromine be cast to use it? Three castings, all fail (Wolfram)

**Casting A — "cube minus square" (Hall directly).** |z³ − 3^y| = 2^x + 5 is a
cube-minus-square Hall difference **iff 3^y is a perfect square, i.e. y even.**
Wolfram: in the Theorem-5 family y = 166317 + 166320·k_y, we have y ≡ 1 (mod 2) (indeed
y ≡ 3 (mod 6)) for **every** k_y — y is **always odd**. So 3^y is never a square; the
direct Hall casting does not apply.

**Casting B — "cube minus cube."** Since y = 3j, |z³ − (3^j)³| = 2^x + 5 is a difference
of two perfect cubes — but then the "nearby cubic" 3^{y/3} = 3^j is a *rational integer*,
not a cubic *irrational*, so Badziahin's machinery (which requires a genuine cubic
irrational ξ) degenerates. This is exactly the already-exhausted factorization approach.

**Casting C — Thue / 2^{2/3} (the Session-3 reframing, the only one yielding a cubic
irrational).** With x = 3a + 2, z³ − 4W³ = 3^y + 5 (W = 2^a), so z/W approximates the
cubic irrational ξ = 2^{2/3} (min. poly t³ − 4, height H = 4). Computed the realized
separation exponent v_real (with |ξ − z/W| ~ W^{−v_real}) across the family (Wolfram):

| k_y | log W | log(3^y+5) | log|ξ − z/W| | v_real |
|-----|-------|-----------|--------------|--------|
| 0   | 76856.9 | 182717.9 | −47854.7 | **+0.623** |
| 1   | 76856.9 | 365439.1 | +134866.5 | **−1.755** |
| 2   | 76856.9 | 548160.3 | +317587.7 | **−4.132** |
| 3   | 76856.9 | 730881.5 | +500308.9 | **−6.510** |

(All with k_x = 1; W = 2^a is fixed by k_x, so log W is constant across the table.)

**Reading:** Badziahin's results live in 2 ≤ v ≤ 3 (and even there only conditionally on
abc/Hall). The Kuromine approximation z/W realizes v_real **far below 2**:
- For the floor case k_y = 0, v_real ≈ 0.623 — this recovers exactly the Session-3
  κ_real ≈ 0.62 (independent confirmation): z/W is within o(1) of 2^{2/3} but is a *bad*
  approximation, below any separation/measure exponent (which are lower bounds, vacuous on
  bad approximations).
- For **every** k_y ≥ 1, v_real is **negative**: |ξ − z/W| ≥ e^{+134867} ≫ 1. The
  "approximation" *diverges* — z/W is not even close to 2^{2/3}. This is a **new** finding
  (Session 3 only computed the k_y = 0 floor). The reason: along the progression 3^y grows
  faster than 2^x (the k_y-increment 166320·log3 ≈ 182720 adds to log h each step while
  log W stays fixed), so (z/W)³ = 4 + (3^y+5)/2^{x−2} blows up; Wolfram gives
  log((3^y+5)/2^{x−2}) ≈ 134869 already at k_y = 1, so (z/W)³ ≈ 3^y/2^{x−2} ≫ 4 and
  z/W ≉ 2^{2/3}.

So the cubic-separation paper cannot apply: the entire family sits at v_real < 1 (one case
near 0.62, all the rest *negative*), whereas the paper's bounds are (i) conditional on
abc/Hall and (ii) nontrivial only for good approximations 2 ≤ v ≤ 3. There is no casting
that places the Kuromine pair (z, 3-something) into the good-approximation cubic regime.

This is the **same two-comparable-S-units root cause** (3^y ≈ z², so 3^y dominates and the
cube-irrational approximation is bad or divergent), now confirmed from the cubic-separation
/ Hall geometry side — the third independent confirmation (after Session 3 irrationality
measures and Session 10 Bugeaud).

### 3. Bajpai effective many-term S-unit equations — assessed

Bajpai's thesis (and Bajpai–Bennett, Acta Arith. 214 (2024), "Effective S-unit equations
beyond 3 terms: Newman's conjecture") gives effective/explicit resolution of S-unit
equations with **many terms**. Two independent reasons it does not apply to Kuromine:

1. **Method = Baker's linear forms in logarithms** (stated in the thesis). This is the
   exact tool RETIRED in Session 5: the relevant p-adic forms have v_p(Λ_p) = 0 (p-adic
   units), so no Baker upper bound can be violated; the archimedean form is too weak by the
   two-S-units regime. Improving constants cannot help.
2. **Requires every term to be an S-unit.** z³ = 2^x + 3^y + 5 has the term z³ where z is
   not a {2,3}-S-unit (z = 4 in the known solution (5,3,4); z > 2^{x/3} with other prime
   factors for Theorem-5). The "+5" is also not a 2,3-S-unit. This is the identical
   obstruction recorded in Session 8 for arXiv:2505.19141. No new content.

## Results

**New results (this session):**

1. **Badziahin arXiv:2509.01105 (Results in Math. 2026) is NOT APPLICABLE.** Its
   cubic-irrational separation bounds are (i) conditional on abc/Hall and (ii) nontrivial
   only in 2 ≤ v ≤ 3. Kuromine cannot be placed there: "cube minus square" fails (y always
   odd in the family); "cube minus cube" degenerates (3^j rational, not a cubic irrational);
   and the 2^{2/3}-Thue casting realizes v_real ≈ 0.62 (floor, k_y=0) or **v_real < 0**
   (all k_y ≥ 1, the approximation *diverges*).

2. **Sharpening of Session 3:** the realized cubic-approximation exponent v_real (= κ_real)
   is **not** a single number ≈ 0.62; it is 0.62 only for the family floor k_y = 0 and goes
   **negative** for every k_y ≥ 1 (−1.75, −4.13, −6.51, …). For almost the entire Theorem-5
   family, z/W does not approximate 2^{2/3} at all — it diverges. (Wolfram-verified table.)

3. **Bajpai many-term effective S-unit work is NOT APPLICABLE** — Baker-based (retired
   Session 5) and requires S-unit-controlled terms, which z³ and the constant 5 are not
   (same obstruction as Session 8 / arXiv:2505.19141).

**Negative results:** No contradiction. The most on-target unassessed paper to date
(Badziahin's cubic-vs-rational separation, the geometry directly underlying |z³ − 3^y|)
sits behind the same barrier, *and is conditional on abc anyway* — and Session 8 already
showed Kuromine's abc-quality is ≈ 0.888 < 1, so abc gives no leverage even if granted.

## Conclusion

Session 11 closes the cubic-irrationality / cube-vs-rational *separation* avenue —
including its newest and most directly relevant instance (Badziahin 2026) — as a third
independent confirmation of the two-comparable-S-units barrier. The key new fact is that the
barrier is even more severe than Session 3 recorded: along the Theorem-5 progression the
cubic approximation z/W to 2^{2/3} is good-ish only at the single floor case (v ≈ 0.62) and
*diverges* for every other member (v < 0). Separation bounds — lower bounds nontrivial only
for good approximations 2 ≤ v ≤ 3 — therefore have nothing to act on, and are conditional on
abc/Hall regardless, while Kuromine's abc-quality 0.888 < 1 means abc grants no leverage.

The fundamental barrier is unchanged. The precise open target stands exactly where Session 10
left it: an **unconditional effective bound with approximation exponent below ~1.1** in the
two-comparable-S-units regime — a tool that does not exist in the 2024–2026 literature, and
which the newest cubic-separation results (conditional, good-approximation-only) do not supply.
