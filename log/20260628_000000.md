### B. Lemma C — the decisive structural reduction (Wolfram)

I attacked Lemma C directly. The result is an **exact identity** that collapses the entire
"dial-orbit discrepancy" question onto already-settled ground.

**Step 1 (exact identity, Wolfram `Simplify` = 0).** For all $x,y\ge0$,
$$\big(27\cdot 2^x + 3^{y+3} + 135\big)^{1/3} \;=\; 3\,\big(2^x + 3^y + 5\big)^{1/3}.$$
Hence
$$\boxed{\ \Theta(x,y) := \operatorname{dist}\big(R_y(x),\,3\mathbb Z\big) \;=\; 3\cdot
\operatorname{dist}\big((2^x+3^y+5)^{1/3},\,\mathbb Z\big).\ }$$
**The dial-orbit defect from $3\mathbb Z$ is exactly 3 times the distance of the original cube
root $z=(2^x+3^y+5)^{1/3}$ from the integers.** The "dial reformulation" did not create a new
analytic object — the $\times 27$ / $W=3z$ rescaling is undone by the cube root, returning the
original cube-distance. This is the single most important finding of the session.

**Step 2 (the family residue, Wolfram).** On the Theorem-5 lattice $x = 5 + 332640\,k_x$, and
$5\equiv 2$, $332640\equiv 0 \pmod 3$, so $x \equiv 2 \pmod 3$ universally. Therefore
$2^x = 4\,M^3$ with $M = 2^{(x-2)/3}\in\mathbb Z$ — a perfect cube times 4. (Verified
$x\bmod 3 = 2$ for $k_x=0,\dots,5$.)

**Step 3 (reduction to the $2^{2/3}$ approximation).** In the $2^x$-dominant survival wedge,
$N = 2^x+3^y+5 = 4M^3 + (3^y+5)$, and the nearest integer to $z = N^{1/3}$ is $m\approx
4^{1/3}M = 2^{2/3}M$, with
$$\operatorname{dist}(z,\mathbb Z) \;\approx\; \frac{|N - m^3|}{3\,N^{2/3}}
\;=\; \frac{|m^3 - 4M^3 - (3^y+5)|}{3\,N^{2/3}}.$$
The minimal achievable numerator is governed by $|m^3 - 4M^3|$ — the **rational approximation
of $4^{1/3} = 2^{2/3}$ by $m/M$**, $M = 2^{(x-2)/3}$ a power of two. This is **exactly the
Session-3 auxiliary $2^{2/3}$-Thue casting** ($z^3 - 4W^3$, $W=2^a$).

**Step 4 (numerical confirmation of decay, Wolfram).** Sweeping $m=\operatorname{round}
(4^{1/3}\,2^a)$ for $a=2,\dots,40$ and computing $\operatorname{gap}=m^3-2^{3a+2}$ and
$\operatorname{dist}\approx|\operatorname{gap}|/(3N^{2/3})$:
- Typical $\log_M|\operatorname{gap}|\approx 2.0$–$2.1$ (generic spacing), but at **deep
  convergents** of $2^{2/3}$ the exponent drops sharply: $a=11$ ($x=35$) gives
  $m=3251$, $\operatorname{gap}=m^3-2^{35}=83883$, $\log_M|\operatorname{gap}|=1.487$, and
  $\operatorname{dist}\approx 0.00265$.
- The corresponding fixed-$y$ sweep ($y=3$, $x\equiv 2\bmod 3$) independently produced the same
  small values ($x=35\!:0.00264$, then $0.00529,0.0106,0.0212,\dots$), and for $x\equiv0\bmod3$
  (NOT the family residue) the distance plunges to $\sim10^{-7}$ ($2^x$ itself a cube). So the
  discrepancy demonstrably descends toward $0$ along convergent subsequences — it is **not**
  bounded below by any positive constant.

**Step 5 (the realized exponent is sub-Dirichlet, Wolfram).** The realized approximation
exponent $\kappa$ of $m/M\to 2^{2/3}$ along the survival family,
$\kappa = (2\log M - \log(3^y+5) + \log(3\cdot 4^{2/3}))/\log M$:
| $(k_x,k_y)$ | $\kappa$ | regime |
|---|---|---|
| $(1,0)$ | $-0.377$ | anti-approx (diverges) |
| $(2,0)$ | $+0.811$ | approx, but $\ll 2$ |
| $(3,0)$ | $+1.208$ | approx, but $\ll 2$ |

Every value is **strictly below the Dirichlet floor $2$** — for most pairs negative (the
