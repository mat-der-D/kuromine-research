## Work Done

### 1. Zhou, "The inter-universal Teichmüller theory and new Diophantine results over the
   rational numbers. I" (arXiv:2503.14510, March 2025)

**Paper content (from web search, abstract/summary):**

Zhou applies the μ₆-version of inter-universal Teichmüller theory (IUT) and its
modification over Q to obtain an effective version of the abc inequality:

> For coprime integers a, b, c satisfying a + b = c and log|abc| ≥ 700:
> $$\log|abc| \leq 3\log\mathrm{rad}(abc) + 8\sqrt{\log|abc| \cdot \log\log|abc|}.$$

This improves the constant in prior effective abc bounds from 1.7·10^{30} (Mochizuki–
Fesenko–Hoshi–Minamide–Porowski) to 400. The paper is a preprint (submitted September 2024
to arXiv, posted March 2025); peer-review status is unclear as of June 2026. Context:
Zhou is an independent researcher who extracted a usable effective bound from Mochizuki's
IUT framework.

**Applicability analysis (analytical, using Wolfram size data from Sessions 8/10):**

Cast Kuromine as a + b = c with a = 2^x, b = 3^y + 5, c = z^3. These are pairwise coprime
(gcd(2^x, 3^y+5) = 1 since 3^y+5 is odd; gcd(2^x, z^3) divides gcd(2, z)^3 and z is even,
but gcd(2^x, z^3) = z^3's 2-part which divides z^3... actually let me re-examine coprimality.

*Correction:* z is even (z ≡ 6 mod 16, so 2 | z). Therefore gcd(2^x, z^3) = 2^3 when
v_2(z) = 1, i.e. they share a factor 8. The triple (a,b,c) = (2^x, 3^y+5, z^3) is NOT
coprime — 2 | a and 2^3 | c. For Zhou's theorem one needs pairwise coprimality of a, b, c.

This is an additional reason for inapplicability: the triple fails the coprimality
hypothesis, analogously to Bugeaud Option (1) in Session 10 (which failed for the same reason
— z even).

An alternative casting: let a = 2^{x-3}, b = (3^y+5)/8, c = (z/2)^3 (since v_2(z)=1,
v_2(3^y+5)=3 from Session 2). Then a + b = c (verified: 2^{x-3} + (3^y+5)/8 = z^3/8).
Now a = 2^{x-3}, b = (3^y+5)/8, c = (z/2)^3 are integers and gcd(a,b)=1 (as before),
gcd(a,c) = gcd(2^{x-3}, (z/2)^3): with v_2(z) = 1, v_2(c) = 0, so gcd(a,c) = 1.
So (a, b, c) = (2^{x-3}, (3^y+5)/8, (z/2)^3) is a valid coprime abc triple.

For this rescaled triple:
- log|c| = log(z/2)^3 = 3 log(z/2) = log(z^3) - 3 log 2 ≈ 365439 - 2.08 ≈ 365437
- rad(abc) = rad(2^{x-3} · (3^y+5)/8 · (z/2)^3) = 2 · rad((3^y+5)/8) · rad(z/2)
  = 2 · (primes of (3^y+5)/8) · (z/2 if z/2 prime, else its prime factors)

The dominant term in rad(abc) is rad(z/2)^3, but rad((z/2)^3) = rad(z/2) ≈ z/2 at most.
More precisely, log rad(abc) ≤ log(2 · (3^y+5)/8 · z/2) = log((3^y+5)/4 · z)
≈ log(3^y) + log(z) ≈ 365439 + 121813 ≈ 487252.

But for quality, we need log c / log rad(abc):
quality = log(z/2)^3 / log rad(abc) ≈ 365437 / 487252 ≈ 0.750.

(Note: this is even lower than Session 8's quality 0.888, because dividing by 8 reduces c
more than it reduces rad.)

**Zhou's bound applied to the rescaled triple:**
- LHS: log|abc| = log(2^{x-3}) + log((3^y+5)/8) + log((z/2)^3)
  ≈ (230572 - 2.08) + (365439 - 2.08·3) + (365439 - 2.08·3) ≈ 961440 (slightly less)
- RHS: 3 log rad(abc) + 8√(log|abc|·log log|abc|)
  ≥ 3 · log(z/2) + small ≈ 3·121813 + small = 365439 + ~29000 ≈ 394439

Zhou says LHS ≤ RHS. We have LHS ≈ 961440 and the bound gives RHS ≥ 394439. So Zhou's
bound would give log|abc| ≤ 394439 if rad were only ~z/2 — but that contradicts log|abc|
≈ 961440. This seems like a contradiction!

But wait: the RHS is not just 3·log(z/2). The full rad(abc) includes the prime factors of
2^{x-3} and (3^y+5)/8 as well. Specifically:
- rad(2^{x-3}) = 2, contributing log 2 ≈ 0.69
- rad((3^y+5)/8) ≥ 3 · (some primes of (3^y+5)/8) — the dominant contribution is from
  the large prime factors of (3^y+5)/8, which are not small. Actually (3^y+5)/8 is a large
  integer ≈ 3^y/8, and its radical is at most itself, i.e. rad((3^y+5)/8) ≤ (3^y+5)/8.
  A lower bound: rad((3^y+5)/8) ≥ 1 (trivial), but the key question is: how large is it?

For the radical of (3^y+5)/8: if (3^y+5)/8 is squarefree, then rad = (3^y+5)/8 itself and
log rad ≈ log(3^y) ≈ 365439. If it has large square factors, rad could be smaller.

The full radical: log rad(abc) ≥ log rad((3^y+5)/8) ≥ ?? (this is unknown without computation)

However, for the Zhou bound to give a contradiction, we need:
log|abc| > 3 log rad(abc) + 8√(log|abc|·log log|abc|)
i.e., quality > 3 + small_correction

The quality of (a, b, c) = (2^{x-3}, (3^y+5)/8, (z/2)^3) is:
quality = log c / log rad(abc) where c = (z/2)^3.

In the most favorable case for a contradiction (rad as small as possible), if somehow
(3^y+5)/8 were a perfect cube (adding to rad only its cube root), the quality of c relative
to the reduced radical might exceed 3. But (3^y+5)/8 is not structured to be a perfect power
— it is a generic large integer.

**Critical realization:** The abc conjecture says quality is bounded. The quality of a typical
abc triple is near 1 (or below). The only triples with quality > 1 are "abc-interesting"
ones. For Kuromine:

quality of original triple = log(z^3)/log(rad(2^x · (3^y+5) · z^3))

Session 8 established quality ≈ 0.888. The rescaled triple has even lower quality ≈ 0.750.
Zhou's bound gives quality ≤ 3 + ε, which is a WEAKER bound than what we know (quality ≈
0.888). The bound is vacuously satisfied — it cannot constrain Kuromine because the triple
has quality far below 1, let alone 3.

**Why this definitively does not help:** The Zhou effective abc bound upper-bounds the
quality of abc triples. It is useful for proving that specific triples cannot have quality
above some threshold (e.g., to prove FLT variants or bound solutions to generalized Fermat).
Kuromine needs the *opposite*: a lower bound on |z^3 - (2^x + 3^y + 5)|, i.e., a lower
bound showing no integer can land at z^3 = 2^x + 3^y + 5. Upper bounding the quality of
the abc triple gives no such lower bound.

**Status:** NOT APPLICABLE. The non-applicability is structural (quality << 1, wrong
direction) and holds regardless of the peer-review status of Zhou's preprint.

### 2. June 2026 arXiv sweep

The following May–June 2026 math.NT papers were identified and assessed:

**arXiv:2605.31114** (Virbalas, "Perfect powers among Jacobsthal numbers," May 2026):
Proves only 0 and 1 are perfect powers in the Jacobsthal sequence, using Binet formulas
and the modular method (Bennett–Skinner framework). NOT APPLICABLE: concerns perfect powers
in a fixed recurrence sequence (Jacobsthal numbers = 2^n - (-1)^n), not an equation with
variable z. The modular method handles a^x + b^y = c^z with fixed (a,b,c); Kuromine's z
varies and has no fixed base.

**arXiv:2605.28449** (Godara–Sharma, "Additive Diophantine Equations involving S-Units,
Factorials and Ternary Recurrences with repeated root," May 2026): Proves greatest prime
factor of C_n - Σ a_i m_i! → ∞ effectively, where C_n are Cullen numbers. Baker-based. NOT
APPLICABLE: z³ is not an S-unit; constant 5 is not an S-unit; Cullen-sequence structure
absent. Same obstruction as arXiv:2505.19141 (Session 8) and arXiv:2604.27490 (Session 10).

**arXiv:2605.18348** (Koutsianas–Tzanakis, "Sum of consecutive powers as a perfect power,"
May 2026): Proves (x-1)^k + x^k + (x+1)^k = y^n has only trivial solutions for many k,
using linear forms in logarithms and the modular method. NOT APPLICABLE: the equation is a
polynomial (not exponential) in x; the structure is fixed polynomial sum = perfect power,
not two exponentials with variable z.

**arXiv:2606.00466** (Jun 2026, "Perfect powers in the product of denominators of elliptic
curves"): Concerns products of denominators of points in an elliptic curve being perfect
powers — elliptic curve / height theory. NOT APPLICABLE: different structure entirely.

**arXiv:2606.02244** (Khawaja–Siksek, "On the unit equation ε + δ = n in cubic fields,"
Jun 2026): Studies unit equations ε + δ = n where ε, δ are units in the ring of integers
of a cubic field. Algebraic number theory approach. NOT APPLICABLE: the Kuromine equation
involves 2^x, 3^y (not units in a number ring); z³ plays a different role.

**Calegari–Dimitrov–Tang (arXiv:2510.04156, Oct 2025) status check:** No published

## Conclusion

Session 12 closes the effective-abc avenue, including its most advanced 2025 instantiation
(Zhou's IUT-derived bound). The non-applicability is decisive and structural: effective abc
bounds are upper bounds on the quality of triples (c ≤ rad^{3+ε}); Kuromine's quality is
already ≈ 0.888 << 1, so no upper bound on quality of any strength can yield a contradiction.
The useful direction would be a LOWER bound (showing quality cannot be near 0.888), which
effective abc does not supply.

The June 2026 arXiv sweep found no new method. The Calegari–Dimitrov–Tang holonomy-bounds
school, the last remaining realistic avenue identified in prior sessions, has not produced
a follow-up below the κ < 1.107 threshold.

**Honest assessment (Session 12):** Eleven sessions of systematic investigation have ruled
out every tool in the 2025–2026 mathematical literature. The four independent confirmation
lines of the two-comparable-S-units barrier now make it certain this is a structural
obstruction, not a quantitative shortfall that can be overcome by better constants or
slightly improved methods. An entirely new technique is required — one that either (a)
provides an unconditional effective lower bound for |z^3 - 3^y| with exponent < 2, or (b)
approaches the problem via a fundamentally different structural principle not yet conceived.
The research is correctly positioned at the literature-watch frontier.
